Launching a new OTT streaming platform in Spain

Spain's streaming market is maturing fast — but there is still a significant gap at the premium local end. While Mediaset Infinity and AtresPlayer anchor the free-to-air digital space, no platform has yet claimed the role of Spain's go-to premium local streaming destination. With ~49M people, 90% broadband penetration, and audiences underserved in regional languages and niche sports, the opportunity is real — but so is the complexity. This framework maps the 6 key dimensions you need to get right.

Content is the #1 retention driver

Content quality and relevance drive subscribe, stay, and churn decisions more than price or UX. A new OTT must define its content identity before anything else.

Strategic positioning options

  • Local-first: Spanish & regional originals — one of the clearest gaps in the market
  • Genre specialist: crime, horror, kids, docs
  • Live sport add-on: niche sports + women's football
  • Aggregator: licensed international catalogue

Spanish originals High priority

Proven genres in Spain
Crime & thriller Romantic comedy Historical drama Horror True crime docs

Co-produce with regional broadcasters (TV3, ETB, TVG) to reduce cost and earn cultural credibility. Regional language content (Catalan, Basque, Galician) unlocks underserved audiences and may attract public subsidies.

Sports rights

Affordable entry points
Padel (fast-growing in Spain) Liga F (women's football) Motorsport LaLiga (very expensive)

LaLiga rights are shared between DAZN and Movistar+ under a costly rights structure. Niche sports with passionate fanbases (padel, esports, cycling) can offer better cost-per-subscriber for a new entrant.

International catalogue

License proven international content to fill catalogue depth while originals ramp up. Prioritise content with Spanish dubbing/subtitles already available. Latin American content performs well — familiar language, lower licensing costs than US studio content.

Content budget guidance

Rough cost ranges
  • Spanish drama series (8 ep): €2–5M
  • Co-production with regional TV: €0.8–2M
  • Licensed international title: €20–200k/yr
  • Documentary: €200–600k
  • Live sport (niche): €100–500k/yr

Youth & mobile-first (18–30)

Consume primarily on mobile. Expect short-form content, social sharing, offline downloads, and low price points. Freemium mobile-only tier (AVOD) works as an acquisition funnel. Real competitors for attention: TikTok and YouTube — think about what you uniquely offer.

Short-form content AVOD entry tier Social features Offline downloads

Regional language audiences

Catalonia, Basque Country, and Galicia each have publicly funded regional broadcasters and loyal local audiences. A new OTT with strong Catalan or Basque originals could win institutional support, subsidies, and a base that larger platforms often under-serve.

~10M Catalan speakers ~750k Basque speakers ~3M Galician speakers

Older audiences (55+)

Significantly underserved by current streaming platforms. Strong appetite for Spanish drama, historical content, and dubbed international films. Price-sensitive but loyal once subscribed. Prefer TV screen experience. Worth a dedicated content lane and simplified UX.

Key behavioural insights

  • Binge-watching peaks Thu–Sun evenings
  • Summer drops off — offer flexible pause/freeze
  • High account sharing (especially 18–28 cohort)
  • Spanish dubbing is often preferred over subtitles by mainstream audiences
  • Strong word-of-mouth for local drama hits

Mediaset Infinity Local AVOD leader Relaunched June 2025

Who they are

The digital streaming arm of Mediaset España — owner of Telecinco, Cuatro, and associated channels. Formerly known as Mitele (launched 2011), it rebranded as Mediaset Infinity on 24 June 2025, adopting the brand and technology architecture of its Italian parent MFE-MediaForEurope. The platform is free and AVOD-first, with a paid tier called Infinity+ (formerly MitelePlus) for ad-free viewing, offline downloads, and broader catalogue access.

Strengths
  • Deep catalogue of mass-market Spanish content: Gran Hermano, La Isla de las Tentaciones, Supervivientes, La que se avecina, First Dates — titles with proven, loyal audiences
  • Free-to-air brand recognition from Telecinco and Cuatro — massive built-in awareness
  • New-generation technology stack co-developed with Mediaset Italia over 18+ months — modern recommendation engine, personalised UX, scalable architecture
  • Ad Infinity: a sophisticated programmatic ad suite (AI-driven ad load control, pause ads, Smart TV formats) — strong monetisation potential for the AVOD model
  • Exclusive digital content and originals created for the platform — not just catch-up TV
  • International offer via Infinity+ Internacional for the Spanish diaspora
Weaknesses / gaps
  • Brand historically associated with mass-market entertainment — may struggle to attract premium or younger quality-seeking subscribers
  • Infinity+ paid tier remains smaller than Atresplayer, which reported 700k+ subscribers in H1 2025
  • Limited regional language (Catalan, Basque, Galician) content investment
  • No live sports rights — a gap vs Movistar+ and DAZN
Strategic direction (2025+)

Fully committed to the AVOD + SVOD hybrid model. Expanding original digital productions, video podcast formats, short-form vertical video, and AI-powered personalisation. The shared MFE architecture may give it a technology edge over some legacy local competitors.

AVOD-first Mass entertainment MFE tech stack Strong ad monetisation Gap: premium originals Gap: regional languages

AtresPlayer Local SVOD challenger

Who they are

The streaming platform of Atresmedia (Antena 3, La Sexta). Free catch-up since 2013; paid Premium tier launched 2019. Closed H1 2025 with 700k+ subscribers, confirming it as Spain's clearest local premium streaming success story.

Strengths
  • Clearest local SVOD success story in Spain — steady subscriber growth
  • Original fiction-first strategy: leads Spanish series launches (FoQ revival, Mar Afuera, Drag Race Spain)
  • Strong content partnerships: Disney+ bundle integration; ViX (TelevisaUnivision) launched on platform Jan 2025 — adds 15,000h of Latin content
  • Growing international presence (LatAm, US Hispanic via Amazon Channels, YouTube TV)
Weaknesses
  • Still relatively small at 700k+ subs — well below Netflix/Disney scale
  • Dependent on Antena 3 brand equity — weaker with younger, digital-native users
  • No significant sports rights
700k+ subscribers (H1 2025) SVOD originals-led ViX partnership (LatAm content)

Netflix Global leader

Strong brand, large catalogue, global originals, recommendation engine. Price sensitivity is a risk in Spain, especially after price increases. Limited investment in Spain's regional-language content. No meaningful live sports offer in Spain.

Movistar+ Telco bundle

LaLiga + Champions League rights, Spanish originals, and strong Telefónica distribution. Major bundle advantage, but still a premium-priced offer with a more complex legacy UX than pure-play OTT rivals.

DAZN

Sports-focused. Partial LaLiga rights, boxing, motorsport. Retention can be challenging outside die-hard sports fans. Limited non-sports content. Potential licensing or partnership angle.

Max & Disney+

Strong US studio content and franchises. Limited Spanish originals investment. Disney+ has family lock-in; Max has prestige drama. Both vulnerable to churn when hit series end.

RTVE Play (free)

Public broadcaster platform with a large Spanish archive, news, and some regional-language content. Not a direct paid competitor but relevant as a strong free Spanish content benchmark.

The real gap — your opportunity

What is still missing in Spain

Mediaset Infinity leads in mass-market AVOD. Atresplayer is the clearest local SVOD success story but remains modest in scale. There is still room in regional language content, niche sports (padel, Liga F), and the 55+ audience. A premium local streaming position — one that feels authentically Spanish across regions, not just Madrid — is still contestable.

User-facing layer

Client apps

Web · iOS · Android · Smart TV

TV screens account for a large share of OTT viewing in Spain. Prioritise Smart TV + web + Android for launch; iOS next.

React / Next.js (web) Smart TV (Tizen, webOS, Android TV) React Native (iOS + Android) Apple TV / Fire TV Offline downloads (DRM-protected) EU Accessibility Act compliance

Recommendation: launch web + Smart TV + Android simultaneously

Video player

Playback · DRM · ABR

The player is your most critical piece of engineering. A buffering spinner or DRM failure loses a subscriber permanently.

Shaka Player (DASH/Widevine) hls.js (iOS/Safari) EZDRM multi-DRM SaaS Widevine + FairPlay + PlayReady AV1 codec (material compression gains, device support permitting) Low-latency live (<3s LL-DASH)

Recommendation: Shaka Player + EZDRM multi-DRM SaaS

Delivery layer

CDN & video delivery

Latency · scale · cost

CDN cost is one of the biggest infrastructure expenses at scale. Cloudflare has strong PoPs in Madrid and Barcelona — attractive for a startup. Multi-CDN or premium CDN options such as Akamai become more relevant as scale and reliability requirements grow.

Cloudflare Stream + Workers Akamai (at scale) AWS CloudFront + MediaPackage Multi-CDN failover (scale)

Recommendation: start with Cloudflare Stream, add origin shield

Transcoding & encoding

VOD · live · quality ladders

Target at least 5 quality levels: 240p, 480p, 720p, 1080p, 4K. Store source files permanently. Consider HDR (HDR10) for premium 4K content where the device base justifies it.

Mux (fastest to market) AWS MediaConvert (cheaper at scale) FFmpeg self-hosted Per-title encoding (QVBR)

Recommendation: Mux for speed to market, MediaConvert to scale

Backend layer

Backend services

API · auth · catalogue · billing

Two Spain-specific priorities: Bizum integration (29M+ active users; omitting it may hurt conversion) and concurrent stream limits enforced via Redis (to reduce account-sharing leakage).

GraphQL API gateway Auth0 / Cognito (never build your own) Stripe + Bizum (via Redsys) Contentful / Sanity (headless CMS) Redis (session / stream concurrency) Catalogue rights service

Recommendation: GraphQL + Auth0 + Stripe/Redsys with Bizum

Data & personalisation

Recommendations · analytics · ML

Personalisation can become a moat that catalogue size alone cannot buy. Thumbnail A/B testing is often high-ROI, and the right artwork can materially improve click-through.

AWS Personalize (recommendation ML) Typesense / Elasticsearch (search) Segment (event bus) BigQuery / Redshift (warehouse) GrowthBook (A/B testing)
Infrastructure layer

Cloud infrastructure

Hosting · DB · ops

AWS opened the eu-south-2 (Spain/Aragón) region in late 2022. It can be a strong option for local data residency and latency, but GDPR compliance depends on governance and processing controls as much as region choice. Start with 3–5 services max, not 30.

AWS eu-south-2 (Spain data residency) PostgreSQL (RDS/Aurora) + Redis Terraform + GitHub Actions (IaC + CI/CD) Kubernetes EKS

Recommendation: AWS eu-south-2 for data + EKS + Terraform from day one

SVOD (subscription)

Monthly or annual recurring subscription. Predictable revenue, highest LTV. Spain's market is price-sensitive — keep base tier under €8/month. Annual plans reduce churn significantly (offer 2 months free).

Basic: €5–7/mo (ad-supported) Standard: €9–11/mo (ad-free) Premium: €13–15/mo (4K, 4 screens)

AVOD (ad-supported free)

Free tier with ads. Excellent for youth acquisition and top-of-funnel growth. To attract advertisers at meaningful scale, strong reach and targeting capability matter. Ads must be managed carefully — intrusive pre-rolls hurt retention.

Best for: under-25 acquisition Spanish CPMs: €5–15

TVOD (pay-per-view)

Pay per title or per live event. Strong for premium live sport moments (boxing, F1 races) and day-and-date film releases. Not a primary revenue model but useful as a revenue booster for tent-pole events.

Telco bundle

Partner with a Spanish telco (Vodafone, Movistar, MasOrange) to bundle your service with broadband or mobile plans. One of the fastest subscriber-acquisition routes. Telcos get content differentiation; you get distribution and reduced churn. Negotiate carefully — telcos may want exclusivity periods.

Vodafone ES Orange Spain MásMóvil / Yoigo

Hybrid SVOD + AVOD tier

Now the market standard (Netflix, Disney+, Max all offer ad-supported tiers). Maximises total addressable market — price-sensitive users get AVOD, quality-seekers pay for ad-free. Build AVOD capability from the start even if you don't launch it on day one.

Churn reduction tactics

  • Annual plan discount (2 months free)
  • Summer pause option (keep subscriber, pause billing)
  • Win-back campaigns at cancellation
  • Student pricing (€3–4/mo) for 18–25 cohort
  • Family plan (up to 5 profiles, one bill)

EU AVMSD (Audiovisual Media Services Directive)

EU-wide obligation

Requires at least 30% European works in on-demand catalogues. Applies to OTT services operating in or targeting EU member states. Those works must also be prominently offered — meaning visible and discoverable, not buried.

Non-compliance: infringement proceedings

Spanish Ley General de Comunicación Audiovisual (2022)

Spain-specific
  • Spain-specific language obligations apply on top of the EU quota; part of the required European works reserve must be available in one of Spain's official languages
  • Revenue-based obligation to finance European audiovisual works, which may be met through direct investment, rights acquisition, and/or contributions to approved funds
  • Additional obligations may apply depending on service type, turnover, and how the service is established or targets Spain
  • Content rating and parental controls mandatory
Engage a Spanish media law firm early

GDPR + Spanish LOPDGDD

Data protection
  • Explicit consent for data collection and profiling
  • Right to erasure, portability, access
  • Data residency: keep user data within the EU where appropriate, alongside proper contractual, security, and governance controls
  • Cookie consent banner required (AEPD guidelines)
  • DPO (Data Protection Officer) required if large-scale profiling

Content licensing & rights

Territory-by-territory clearance
  • Rights must be cleared territory by territory; Spain may be licensed alone or together with other territories depending on the deal
  • Music rights: SGAE (Spanish) + CISAC network for international content
  • Subtitling/dubbing rights must be explicit in content contracts
  • Windowing rules depend on release strategy, financing, and rights agreements; Spanish and EU rules can affect the theatrical-to-streaming timeline

EU Accessibility Act (EAA) — 2025

Became applicable from June 2025. Requires digital services, including relevant streaming experiences, to address accessibility through measures such as captions, audio description, screen reader compatibility, and accessible player controls where applicable. Build this in from day one — retrofitting is expensive.

Applicable from June 2025

Anti-piracy obligations

  • Implement DRM on all content (Widevine + FairPlay + PlayReady)
  • Token-authenticated CDN URLs — prevent hotlinking
  • Maintain appropriate privacy, security, and notice-handling processes alongside anti-piracy operations
  • Work with rights holders, industry bodies, and legal counsel on notice-and-enforcement processes appropriate to Spain
