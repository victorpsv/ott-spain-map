Interactive concept map - OTT platform strategy for Spain

Spain’s streaming market is maturing fast, yet key gaps remain — particularly in premium local content. Platforms like Mediaset Infinity and AtresPlayer anchor the free-to-air digital space, while global players dominate subscription streaming, leaving room for further innovation in premium local streaming experiences. With ~48M people, high broadband penetration, and audiences underserved in regional languages and niche sports, the opportunity is clear — but execution is complex. This interactive concept map explores the 6 strategic dimensions required to design, build, and scale an OTT platform in Spain.

Strategic dimensions

Prioritise execution quality. Fast apps, stable playback, and reliable platform performance have a direct impact on retention and churn.

Match payments to the business model. Use card-on-file and digital wallets for subscriptions, and support local methods like Bizum where they add value for one-off purchases and promotions.

Define a distinctive content strategy early. Content remains the main retention driver, so originals, rights, and catalogue positioning should be set before scaling the platform.

Plan for regulation from day one. Quotas, accessibility, privacy, and audiovisual funding obligations should be built into the operating model from the start.

Content is the #1 retention driver

Content quality and relevance drive subscribe, stay, and churn decisions more than price or UX. A new OTT must define its content identity before anything else.

Strategic positioning options

  • Local-first: Spanish & regional originals — the biggest gap in the market
  • Genre specialist: crime, horror, kids, docs
  • Live sport add-on: niche sports + women's football
  • Aggregator: licensed international catalogue

Spanish originals High priority

Proven genres in Spain
Crime & thriller Romantic comedy Historical drama Horror True crime docs

Co-produce with regional broadcasters (TV3, ETB, TVG) to reduce cost and earn cultural credibility. Regional language content (Catalan, Basque, Galician) unlocks underserved audiences and may attract public subsidies.

Sports rights

Affordable entry points
Padel (exploding in Spain) Liga F (women's football) Motorsport LaLiga (very expensive)

LaLiga rights are split between DAZN and Movistar+ — prohibitively expensive to compete on directly. Niche sports with passionate fanbases (padel, esports, cycling) offer better cost-per-subscriber.

International catalogue

License proven international content to fill catalogue depth while originals ramp up. Prioritise content with Spanish dubbing/subtitles already available. Latin American content performs well — familiar language, lower licensing costs than US studio content.

Content budget guidance

Indicative ranges
  • Spanish drama series (8 ep): €2–5M
  • Co-production with regional TV: €0.8–2M
  • Licensed international title: €20–200k/yr
  • Documentary: €200–600k
  • Live sport (niche): €100–500k/yr

Indicative ranges based on public industry benchmarks, European production data, and broadcaster disclosures.

Emerging formats & mobile-first content

Short-form and always-on formats are becoming more relevant for discovery, younger audiences, and top-of-funnel acquisition. OTT strategies increasingly combine long-form premium viewing with mobile-native formats.

Shorts (vertical video, <60s) Microdramas (1–5 min episodic) FAST channels Video podcasts

AI can help automate clip extraction, trailer creation, and social-ready edits from longer-form assets, turning existing catalogues into continuous discovery and acquisition funnels.

Youth & mobile-first (18–30)

Consume primarily on mobile. Expect short-form content, social sharing, offline downloads, and low price points. Freemium mobile-only tier (AVOD) works as an acquisition funnel. Real competitors for attention: TikTok and YouTube — think about what you uniquely offer.

Regional language audiences

Catalonia, Basque Country, and Galicia each have publicly funded regional broadcasters and fiercely loyal local audiences. A new OTT with strong Catalan or Basque originals could win institutional support, subsidies, and a base that larger platforms ignore.

~10M Catalan speakers ~750k Basque speakers ~3M Galician speakers

Older audiences (55+)

Significantly underserved by current streaming platforms. Strong appetite for Spanish drama, historical content, and dubbed international films. Price-sensitive but loyal once subscribed. Prefer TV screen experience. Worth a dedicated content lane and simplified UX.

Key behavioural insights

  • Binge-watching peaks Thu–Sun evenings
  • Summer drops off — offer flexible pause/freeze
  • High account sharing (especially 18–28 cohort)
  • Spanish dubbing preferred over subtitles by 55%+
  • Strong word-of-mouth for local drama hits

Mediaset Infinity Local AVOD leader Relaunched June 2025

Who they are

The digital streaming arm of Mediaset España — owner of Telecinco, Cuatro, and associated channels. Formerly known as Mitele (launched 2011), it rebranded as Mediaset Infinity on 24 June 2025, adopting the brand and technology architecture of its Italian parent MFE-MediaForEurope. The platform is free and AVOD-first, with a paid tier called Infinity+ (formerly MitelePlus) for ad-free viewing, offline downloads, and full catalogue access.

Strengths
  • Deep catalogue of mass-market Spanish content: Gran Hermano, La Isla de las Tentaciones, Supervivientes, La que se avecina, First Dates — titles with proven, loyal audiences
  • Free-to-air brand recognition from Telecinco and Cuatro — massive built-in awareness
  • New-generation technology stack co-developed with Mediaset Italia over 18+ months — modern recommendation engine, personalised UX, scalable architecture
  • Ad Infinity: a sophisticated programmatic ad suite (AI-driven ad load control, pause ads, Smart TV formats) — strong monetisation potential for the AVOD model
  • Exclusive digital content and originals created for the platform — not just catch-up TV
  • International offer via Infinity+ Internacional for the Spanish diaspora
  • Strong content partnerships: Movistar, Samsung, Amazon, Google as the most relevant
Strategic direction (2025+)

Covers AVOD, SVOD and TVOD models. Expanding original digital productions, video podcast formats, short-form vertical video, and AI-powered personalisation. The new architecture gives it a technology edge over legacy local competitors.

AVOD-SVOD-TVOD Mass entertainment MFE tech stack Strong ad monetisation

AtresPlayer Local SVOD challenger

Who they are

The streaming platform of Atresmedia (Antena 3, La Sexta). Free catch-up since 2013; paid Premium tier launched 2019. Closed H1 2025 with 708,000 subscribers (+13% YoY), targeting 800k by end of 2025.

Strengths
  • Clearest local SVOD success story in Spain — steady subscriber growth
  • Original fiction-first strategy: leads Spanish series launches (FoQ revival, Mar Afuera, Drag Race Spain)
  • Strong content partnerships: Disney+ bundle integration; ViX (TelevisaUnivision) launched on platform Jan 2025 — adds 15,000h of Latin content
  • Growing international presence (LatAm, US Hispanic via Amazon Channels, YouTube TV)
Weaknesses
  • Still relatively small at ~708k subs — well below Netflix/Disney scale
  • Dependent on Antena 3 brand equity — weaker with younger, digital-native users
  • No significant sports rights
~708k subscribers (H1 2025) SVOD originals-led ViX partnership (LatAm content)

Netflix Global leader

Strong brand, large catalogue, global originals, recommendation engine. Price hike backlash is real — losing price-sensitive subscribers. Under-invests in Spanish regional content. No live sports.

Movistar+ Telco bundle

LaLiga + Champions League rights, Spanish originals, bundled with Telefónica. Huge distribution advantage but tied to Telefónica subscribers, high price point, and a complex legacy UX.

DAZN

Sports-focused. Partial LaLiga rights, boxing, motorsport. Struggling with retention outside die-hard sports fans. Limited non-sports content. Potential licensing/partnership angle.

Max & Disney+

Strong US studio content and franchises. Limited Spanish originals investment. Disney+ has family lock-in; Max has prestige drama. Both vulnerable to churn when hit series end.

RTVE Play (free)

Public broadcaster AVOD. Large Spanish archive, news, regional content. Not a direct paid competitor but sets a quality bar for free Spanish content and is growing fast in digital rankings.

The real gap — your opportunity

What is still missing in Spain

Mediaset Infinity leads AVOD mass entertainment. AtresPlayer leads local SVOD originals but remains relatively small. Neither has meaningfully invested in regional language content, niche sports (padel, Liga F), or the 55+ audience. The premium local streaming position — a platform that feels authentically Spanish across all regions, not just Madrid — is still genuinely available.

Technology is an execution discipline

In OTT, technology choices are less about tools and more about execution quality. Fast apps, stable playback, resilient entitlement systems, and efficient operations directly affect retention, monetisation, and customer support costs. The goal is not to build a perfect stack, but to ship a robust, scalable platform quickly and improve it with real user data.

Time-to-market > perfect architecture Playback quality = retention Data drives product decisions

SVOD (subscription)

Monthly or annual recurring subscription. Predictable revenue, highest LTV. Spain's market is price-sensitive — keep base tier under €8/month. Annual plans reduce churn significantly (offer 2 months free).

Basic: €5–7/mo (ad-supported) Standard: €9–11/mo (ad-free) Premium: €13–15/mo (4K, 4 screens)

AVOD (ad-supported free)

Free tier with ads. Excellent for youth acquisition and top-of-funnel growth. Requires a minimum 2–3M monthly active users to attract Spanish advertisers at competitive CPMs. Ads must be managed carefully — intrusive pre-rolls hurt retention.

Best for: under-25 acquisition Spanish CPMs: €5–15

TVOD (pay-per-view)

Pay per title or per live event. Strong for premium live sport moments (boxing, F1 races) and day-and-date film releases. Not a primary revenue model but useful as a revenue booster for tent-pole events.

Telco bundle

Partner with a Spanish telco (Vodafone ES, Orange, MásMóvil) to bundle your service with broadband or mobile plans. Fastest subscriber acquisition route. Telcos get content differentiation; you get distribution and reduced churn. Negotiate carefully — telcos will want exclusivity periods.

Vodafone ES Orange Spain MásMóvil / Yoigo

Hybrid SVOD + AVOD tier

Now the market standard (Netflix, Disney+, Max all offer ad-supported tiers). Maximises total addressable market — price-sensitive users get AVOD, quality-seekers pay for ad-free. Build AVOD capability from the start even if you don't launch it on day one.

Recommended model for Spain launch

Churn reduction tactics

  • Annual plan discount (2 months free)
  • Summer pause option (keep subscriber, pause billing)
  • Win-back campaigns at cancellation
  • Student pricing (€3–4/mo) for 18–25 cohort
  • Family plan (up to 5 profiles, one bill)

EU AVMSD (Audiovisual Media Services Directive)

EU-wide obligation

Requires at least 30% European content in on-demand catalogues. Applies to any OTT operating in or targeting EU member states. Must be "prominently offered" — i.e. recommended and discoverable, not buried.

Non-compliance: infringement proceedings

Spanish Ley General de Comunicación Audiovisual (2022)

Spain-specific
  • 30% Spanish-language content in catalogue minimum
  • 5% of annual revenue levy payable to ICAA (Spanish Film Institute) for funding national productions
  • Regional language quotas may be required depending on where you are licensed
  • Content rating and parental controls mandatory
Engage a Spanish media law firm early

GDPR + Spanish LOPDGDD

Data protection
  • Explicit consent for data collection and profiling
  • Right to erasure, portability, access
  • Data residency: store Spanish user data in EU (ideally AWS eu-south-2 Spain)
  • Cookie consent banner required (AEPD guidelines)
  • DPO (Data Protection Officer) required if large-scale profiling

Content licensing & rights

Territory-by-territory clearance
  • Rights must be cleared for Spain (and separately for Andorra, Gibraltar if relevant)
  • Music rights: SGAE (Spanish) + CISAC network for international content
  • Subtitling/dubbing rights must be explicit in content contracts
  • Windowing rules: theatrical → SVOD typically 4 months in Spain (post-2022 LGCA)

EU Accessibility Act (EAA) — 2025

Came into force June 2025. Requires OTT platforms to provide accessible services including closed captions, audio description, screen reader compatibility, and accessible player controls for users with disabilities. Build this in from day one — retrofitting is expensive.

Mandatory since June 2025

Anti-piracy obligations

  • Implement DRM on all content (Widevine + FairPlay + PlayReady)
  • Token-authenticated CDN URLs — prevent hotlinking
  • Register with AEPD and cooperate with content protection notices
  • Spain has a dedicated anti-piracy body (FAPAE + PRODEL) — proactive cooperation reduces legal risk
